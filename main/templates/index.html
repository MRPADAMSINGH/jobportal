NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations) play a crucial role in promoting education and addressing educational challenges worldwide. Education is a fundamental human right and a key driver of personal and societal development. Here are some key thoughts and considerations regarding the role of NGOs in education: Helping Hands, through its flagship programme Mission Education, aims to empower underprivileged children by providing education, nutrition, and wellness support. Our program is aligned with the objectives of the New Education Policy (2020) and the Sustainable Development Goal 4 (Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all). Mission Education caters to children (3-18 years) facing various challenges, including those from poor families, differently-abled children, disaster-struck children, abandoned and street children, as well as those living in tribal belts, remote villages, and hard-to-reach areas. In addition to education, we also provide free books and job opportunities, ensuring holistic support for the children's development.

Helping Hands acts as a catalyst in the cycle of change, complementing and supplementing government efforts to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals. We collaborate with like-minded institutions and individuals to implement high-impact programs that facilitate access, enhance quality, and foster long-term behavioral change at the grassroots level. Furthermore, we provide a platform for career advancement, allowing individuals to register for jobs and enabling HR professionals to post job opportunities, thereby bridging the gap between job seekers and employers.
